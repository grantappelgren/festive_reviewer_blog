HARD SUMMER

  • Location: Fontana, CA
  •  
  • Date: August
  •  
  • Duration: 2 Days
  •  
  • Time of Day: Day + Night
  •  
  • Stages: 5
  •  
  • Genre: Various
  •  
  • Host: Insomniac
PRODUCTION

Hard Summer features a couple large open-outdoor stages that are fairly similar to each other (pictured above and below), as well as three simple tent-stages. From a sheer size perspective, the open-outdoor stages are par and very basic in design, with average visuals, lasers, pyrotechnics, steam, and more. The tent-stages are not really unique in their own right, and more of a way to get out of the sun in my opinion (it gets ridiculously hot).

C - Average
VARIETY

This festival offers multiple different genres of electronic dance music, as well as rap/hip-hop. However, it heavily features trap from the electronic dance side. If you are into trap or rap/hip-hop, this is probably a festival for you. There are still other forms of electronic dance music offered, but those artists are a bit few and far between here.

C - Average
ATMOSPHERE

It's hot. Unfortunately, the tents, fans, and mist can only do so much to protect you from the blazing heat of the summer sun. Held at Fontana Speedway, you can feel it from above and rising up from the cement. Additionally, much of the infield is dirt that gets kicked up and creates a great deal of dust making it seem like other festivals use air-purifiers. Aside from the weather and other conditions, Insomniac doesn't really add anything at all in terms of activities, installations, etc. in the common areas. Not the most comfortable experience.

F - Poor
RETURN?

In short, the weather isn't comfortable, there's dust everywhere, the water lines are insanely long, and I'm not the biggest fan of trap or rap/hip-rop. I will not be returning to this festival, as it's just not my cup of tea. I would only recommend it to people who absolutley love the aforementioned genres of music.
