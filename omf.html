PRODUCTION

A single stage festival, OMF is held at the Greek Theatre at UC Berkeley. The stage is average in size but there are really no surrounding additions to make it stand out in any way. The amount of visuals, lasers, pyrotechnics, etc. seemed a bit limited as well. It's not the worst stage in the world, but definitely nothing special in my opinion. There were speaker and other audio issues that also lasted awhile during one of the sets.

D - Below Average
VARIETY

With only five artists making up the lineup, the festival featured a decent variety. This included electronic dance, rap, and alernative music. However, unless specifically going for a certain artist or artists, you may be dissapointed. The problem with only having one stage going at a time is it leaves the attendees with no options other than to watch/listen to the active set. This could obviously cause boredom and impatience.

D - Below Average
ATMOSPHERE

The location doesn't really offer a huge space to walk around or sit down away from the stage. Really, it's just the stage and concession area. The raised seating gives the stage a sunken appearance, which is much different from the typical festival viewpoint, which is usually ground level. One unique aspect of the festival is that it's powered by cryptocurrency, which can be used to redeem items, services, and experiences. This cryptocurrency network may be something that becomes more commonplace at some festivals moving forward, but that remains to be seen. In any event, the overall atmosphere is sub-par when compared to other festivals.

D - Below Average
RETURN?

This was an interesting experience that really didn't do too much for me. Nothing met the standards I'm used to, and I guess spoiled with from Insomniac festivals. I don't plan on returning and wouldn't recommend it.
